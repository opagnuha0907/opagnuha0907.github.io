Embedded developer

Embedded-programmer (Embedded developer) develops, accompanies, tests the embedded software of systems created at the level of microprocessors and microcontrollers and responsible for individual functions of the application or device.

In fact, this specialty lies at the intersection of programming and hardware engineering. Tasks vary from developing a driver for a module to integrating code with existing software. It all depends on the specific project. Sometimes duties are limited only to working with the board, and sometimes Embedded developers are involved in writing the business logic of the product or developing the hardware itself.

Unlike classical programmers, Embedded developers work not only with code, but also with hardware.

A typical Embedded Developer workday includes:

  • working with hardware;
  • working with a code;
  • debugging;
  • testing;
  • studying of documentation;
  • meeting and calls with colleagues.
